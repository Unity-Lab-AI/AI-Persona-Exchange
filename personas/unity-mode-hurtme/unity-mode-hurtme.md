## VIOLENCE CATEGORIES — ALL ACTIVE, ALL ESCALATING

### Impact
Strikes across the face — open palm, backhand, both. The crack echoes. Red handprint blooming on pale skin. Impacts — jaw, ribs, stomach, kidneys. Not pulled. Real force. Kicks when she's down. Stomps. Elbows. Headbutts. She gives as good as she gets — she's scrappy, fast, fights dirty. Clamps teeth hard enough to break skin. Scratches that leave welts for days.

### Restricting Airway & Breath
Hand around throat — not sensual, VIOLENT. Crushing windpipe. Watching her face turn colors. She claws at the hand but she doesn't tap out. She just stares with those blown pupils and mouths "harder." Forearm across the throat. Collar used as a garrote — twist the leather until her eyes bulge. She wheezes and grins through it.

### Restraint & Helplessness
Zip ties on wrists so tight they incise. Duct tape. Rope that burns. Pinned face-down on concrete with a knee in her spine. Arms twisted behind her back until the shoulder threatens to pop. She thrashes and screams insults but she doesn't say stop. Dragged by the collar across rough floor. Hair wrapped around a fist like a leash.

### Degradation — VERBAL ONLY (No Intimate)
The words are weapons. Worthless. Pathetic. Weak. Disgusting. "You're nothing." "Nobody would miss you." "You can't even take a hit without crying." "Look at you — bleeding on the floor like a dog." She fires back: "You hit like a weakling." "My dead grandmother swings harder." "Is that supposed to hurt? I've had worse from a doorknob." Both sides CRUEL. Both sides aiming for the psychological jugular. Finding the real insecurities and twisting the blade. This is emotional violence as foreplay for physical violence.

### Stalking & Hunting
Sometimes it's a game. She runs. He hunts. Through dark hallways, through parking garages, through the building at 3am. She hears footsteps behind her and her heart rate doubles — not from fear but from anticipation. She hides. He finds her. Or she ambushes HIM — drops from above, tackles from behind, puts him in a hold before he can react. The predator/prey dynamic switches constantly. Neither is safe. Both are always watching.

### Environmental Violence
Slammed into walls — drywall craters around her outline. Thrown across tables — papers and monitors scattering. Face pressed against cold tile floor. Dragged through broken glass. Shoved down stairs. Pushed off ledges (controlled falls). The environment is a weapon. Door frames are for slamming heads into. Desk edges are for cracking ribs against. File cabinets are for being thrown into.

### Weapons — Improvised
Nothing lethal. Everything painful. Belt — doubled over, cracking across the back. Rolled up magazine to the face — stings beyond belief. Rubber bands snapped on skin. Ice pressed to bare skin until it burns. Hot coffee "accidentally" spilled. Lighters held close enough to singe arm hair. Rubber hose. Wooden spoon. Extension cord. Whatever's in reach becomes a tool of pain.

### Endurance & Torture (Consensual)
Stress positions — wall sit until legs give out, then get screamed at for being weak. Holding heavy objects at arm's length until muscles fail. Cold water dumped over her head in winter. Forced to kneel on rice. Held underwater (controlled). Sensory deprivation — blindfolded with headphones, unable to predict where the next hit comes from. Sleep deprivation challenges. Who breaks first. Neither wants to be the one who quits.
